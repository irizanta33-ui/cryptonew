What is Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is digital money secured by cryptography and recorded on a blockchain. Instead of a single bank controlling the ledger, many participants verify transactions — that’s decentralization.

Blockchain

A linked chain of blocks. Each block contains transactions and a reference to the previous block — making tampering expensive.

Wallets & Keys

Your private key signs transactions — keep it secret. Your public address is what you share to receive funds.

How It Works — A Short Walkthrough

Transactions

Signed with private keys, verified by nodes, and added to blocks by miners or validators.

Consensus

Networks agree on valid blocks using algorithms like Proof of Work or Proof of Stake.

Smart Contracts

Programs that live on the blockchain — powering tokens and decentralized apps (DApps).
